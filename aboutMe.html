Title-Less, defined as someone having no title

This is a phrase I coined last year after I was in rehab for total knee replacement. I had a lot of time to reflect on my past career path and what the future holds. My ah ha moment came when I was asked by a career coach what certifications do you have with all of your experience and stories?

I realized I was stumped and I began to run through my previous experiences and titles. I started to rattle of titles I believe I have earned or have been assigned:

  • Father
  • Entrepreneur
  • Sales Assassin
  • Shithead
  • Owner
  • Manager
  • Director of Sales & Marketing
  • Founder
  • Umpire in Chief
  • Coach
  • E-commerce expert

I decided to finish my degree.

After 38 years I achieved my undergraduate degree from IU in Criminal Justice with a minor in Forensic Studies last year. Once I accomplished achieving my degree I felt like I needed more. My passion has always been e-commerce but I was missing something. Although I created my first business logo in 1984 on an Imac in Bloomington coupled with being the first Ticket Brokers online in 1997. I actually reviewed the Stubhub business plan. I bought and sold over 400 Super Bowl tickets in 1999 ONLINE including a party hosted by Peyron Manning.

I am still Titleless

My next path was to try getting accepted to the Salesforce "Pathfinder" program. After getting turned down twice I went to plan B. Eventually I tried the free coding class offered on a facebook page and decided to erase that app quickly. Subsequently, after 3 or 4 infomercials, and constant pop up screen from Eleven Fifty I thought maybe I could do this. After learning about the $30,000 investment I decided to pursue other areas and delete that app again.

  • Could I teach myself?
  • Could I try some free classes again?
  • Maybe I can find an alternative!

One of the things I learned in life from my mother is to always keep trying and never quit. If I fall I always try to fall forward and learn from the past. When the events of 9/11 left me unemployed and without an identity or money, I kept chugging. When Covid hit in March I was furloughed and it was time for an immediate change. I was so fortunate to have the opportunity to take advantage of the Next Level jobs grant and soon will find myself with a Eleven Fifty badge and

Earn my title as a Web Developer!
